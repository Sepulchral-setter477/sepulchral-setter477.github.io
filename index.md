## 📁 Managing Your Company Files

Company files contain your financial data. Use these steps to handle your records:

* Creating a file: Select "File" from the top menu, then choose "New Company" to start a fresh set of books.
* Opening a file: Select "Open or Restore Company" to access an existing file stored on your hard drive. 
* Saving progress: Press "Ctrl + S" frequently to save changes to your current active file.
* File location: Keep your company files in a dedicated folder on your computer. Create a shortcut to this folder to save search time.

## 🧮 Performing Bank Reconciliations

Reconciliation ensures your accounting records match your bank statements. This process prevents errors in your finance tracking:

1. Navigate to the "Banking" menu.
2. Select "Reconcile" from the dropdown list.
3. Choose the bank account you want to check.
4. Enter the ending balance and the statement date shown on your bank statement.
5. Compare the transactions in the software to the transactions in your statement.
6. Click the checkbox next to every matching transaction.
7. Confirm that the difference between the statement and your records is zero.
8. Click "Reconcile Now" to finish the task.

## 💾 Setting Up Backup Schedules

Backups protect your data from loss due to system failure. Configure your schedule to store copies of your data safely:

1. Go to the "File" menu and select "Back Up Company."
2. Choose "Create Local Backup."
3. Select the folder where you want to store your back up files. It is best practice to use an external drive or a secure cloud folder.
4. Select "Schedule Backups" to open the automation tools.
5. Choose how often you want the system to save a copy. Daily or weekly intervals serve most small businesses well.
6. Save your settings. The application runs these backups in the background while you work.
